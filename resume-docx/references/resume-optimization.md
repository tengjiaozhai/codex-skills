# Resume Optimization Guide

## Optimize for signal

- Match the target role in the headline and summary.
- Put the most relevant and recent experience first.
- Lead with impact, not responsibility.
- Keep wording specific, credible, and easy to scan.

## Rewrite bullets with evidence

- Prefer `action + scope + method + result`.
- Replace vague verbs like `负责`, `参与`, or `协助` with stronger action verbs when the claim is still truthful.
- Quantify outcomes when the user has numbers. If no numbers exist, state scale, ownership, or frequency instead.
- Remove filler such as `熟练`, `良好沟通能力`, or generic self-praise unless it is tied to evidence.

## Tailor to a job description

- Mirror important keywords from the JD when they are factually supported.
- Reorder bullets so the closest match appears first.
- Trim unrelated tools and low-value details.
- Add missing keywords to the summary, skills, or relevant bullet points instead of keyword stuffing.

## Improve each section

- Summary: 2-3 lines that define seniority, domain, strongest tools, and business impact.
- Experience: 2-5 bullets per role; recent roles should be stronger than older ones.
- Projects: Show why the project matters, what was built, and what changed.
- Skills: Group by category instead of listing a long flat string.
- Education and certifications: Keep concise unless the role depends on them.

## Preserve trust

- Do not invent achievements, metrics, dates, titles, or employers.
- If evidence is weak, rewrite for clarity instead of exaggerating.
- If a claim sounds inflated, soften it and ask for proof only when it materially changes the result.

## Quick review checklist

- The top third of the resume makes the target role obvious.
- Every recent role contains at least one concrete outcome.
- Keywords match the target JD without sounding forced.
- Contact info and links are current.
- Dates, punctuation, and tense are consistent.

## Example

- Weak: `Responsible for backend development and system maintenance.`
- Stronger: `Built and maintained backend services for payment workflows, improving incident recovery speed with clearer observability and runbooks.`
